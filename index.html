Theory of Language
Objective of the method
The general objective is to provide learners with the basic elements of the target language so they can develop near-native fluency and pronunciation.   
The Silent Way is language teaching method developed by the mathematician Caleb Gattegno in 1963.  
The Silent Way is an "artificial approach” based on the principle that a second language is acquired through silent awareness and active trial. 
Theory of Learning
Teacher remains silent most of the time.  Students are the main focus. Cooperation and peer correction is encouraged. 
Teacher’s role
Teachers must change their perception about their own role. Teaching requires non verbal clues to convey meaning. Teachers use non-verbal language, charts and wooden rods for eliciting students answers.  
Students must develop independence, autonomy and responsibility. Students must work cooperatively rather than competitive. Students must be teachers too, problem-solvers and self-evaluators.
